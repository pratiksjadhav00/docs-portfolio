## About me

Coming from a content writing background in the digital advertising industry, I was first exposed to technical writing around two years ago while working on documentation for a finance-domain client.

I quickly adapted to the discipline and demonstrated my ability to deliver high-quality documentation within project deadlines. Through various client engagements, I had the opportunity to collaborate closely with product owners, and those interactions sparked a strong interest in the field of technical writing.

Since then, I have gained hands-on experience with a wide range of technical writing tools, concepts, and documentation types. Today, I work closely with subject matter experts (SMEs), product managers, and developers to create documentation that is structured, accurate, and focused on helping users accomplish their goals effectively.

My experience includes creating API documentation, user guides, installation guides, administrator guides, agent implementation guides, release notes, and online help content. I author documentation using tools and technologies such as MadCap Flare, Adobe RoboHelp, Confluence, DITA, Markdown, and Git.

I am also part of a technical writing mentorship program and community that includes experienced technical writers from around the world. Through this network, I stay informed about developments in software documentation, emerging industry trends, and new tools and practices. This continuous exposure helps me strengthen my skills and accelerate my growth as a technical writer.
